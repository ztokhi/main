My time in Berkeley's intellectual ecosystem taught me how research communities create entirely new fields. In 2015, as an undergrad, I was manually mapping roads in a startup's living room in Albany - infrastructure that would later support Google's first driverless cars, back when autonomous navigation was still theoretical. I watched SkyDeck evolve from similar humble beginnings into a world-class hub for emerging technology. 

My career took an entirely different trajectory. It has been rooted in human rights programming and impact-driven initiatives. However, it maintained a theme of building ethical governance and systems-level transformation for human rights programming. 

These experiences showed me how transformative ideas often start with small, dedicated teams working at the edges of possibility, much like what MATS is pioneering in Berkeley today. 
 
The imperative to address AI alignment risks is uniquely profound. It is a challenge that will shape not just what we can do but what we might become.
